Task 2 - Prime factors

  1. Assuming that you are generating a 1024 bit RSA key and the prime factors have a 512 bit length, what is the probability of picking the same prime factor twice? Explain your answer.
    Hint: How many primes with length 512 bit or less exist?

A 1024-bit key is a result of multiplying two 512-bit primes.

According to the function of counting primes for a 512-bit number, this is about 2512/log(2512) primes, which is 3,778*10^151. But there are infinitely many primes and therefore infinitely many relative primes.

But even if there are only prime numbers, the possibility of choosing the same prime factor twice is about 7 * 10^-304.
